As with most languages, it's essentially impossible to get an exact number of
speakers. Even if you could reach and count every last person, you'd have to
decide who counts as a "speaker," and this is much more difficult than it
sounds. Does someone need to be measurably fluent in the language to count as a
speaker of that language? Maybe they just need to know enough of the language's
words? Or maybe there is a better line somewhere in-between, like day-to-day
conversational skill? How would you even determine if somebody is conversational
in Toki Pona?

Given the complexity of defining a "speaker" of Toki Pona, most efforts to count
speakers have relied on either self-identification or direct study of community
activity. We'll examine both, and try to find a plausible estimate for the
number of Toki Pona speakers.

### Community Surveys

The Toki Pona Census is run every two years by a small group of volunteers, and
when it is run, it runs for six weeks. In this time, members of the community
spread word about the census in
[every known Toki Pona community](https://sona.pona.la/wiki/Communities), giving
it the largest and most complete audience of any community survey.

On top of this, the census is run in August, starting at the same time as
[suno pi toki pona](https://suno.pona.la/). The boost from the event helps the
census reach the most of the community that it can, as quieter or less frequent
participants in the community return to watch and participate in the event.

The most recent community-wide census ran in August 2024. That census received
1,997 responses, of whom 1,596 reported to know Toki Pona. There are a whole lot
more questions worth exploring which the census covers, which you can
[read about in the census results post](https://tokiponacensus.github.io/results2024/).
But for the purpose of counting Toki Pona speakers, this is a good start: There
are no less than ~1,600 people who claim to speak Toki Pona.

But this is only a start: there must be more Toki Pona speakers than the census
can tell us. Consider that the census is primarily distributed online, so it is
unlikely to reach anyone who rarely or never participates in the online
community. Further, the census is voluntary, and so only reaches those who are
interested in taking surveys in the first place. But most importantly, there is
an even better lower bound that actually exceeds the number of respondents on
the census: Community activity!

While we're discussing surveys, it's also worth mentioning the Linku word and
glyph surveys. Rather than attempt to study the breadth of the community, these
surveys attempt to study how the community uses Toki Pona and Sitelen Pona. They
also run in August, but are run every year rather than every other year. In
August 2025,
[the word survey had 971 respondents](https://github.com/lipu-linku/ijo/tree/main/survey/2025),
and
[the glyph survey had 766 respondents](https://github.com/lipu-linku/ijo/tree/main/survey/glyphs-2025).
Different surveys have different reach and appeal to different respondents,
which can significantly change the outcome.

### Community Activity

While not all community activity is _in_ Toki Pona, overall community activity
can tell us how many people have enough interest in Toki Pona to join the
community and to participate in it. That can provide a decent upper bound on the
total number of Toki Pona speakers. So, we'll look at the three biggest and most
active Toki Pona communities:

As of May 2026, [ma pona pi toki pona on Discord](https://discord.gg/mapona) has
just shy of 20,000 members, with 1,500-3,000 users reading the server each week,
and 200-400 users messaging or voice chatting each week. You can dig into these
stats yourself in
[the server's Insights dashboard](https://discord.com/developers/servers/301377942062366741/analytics/engagement?interval=2&start=2026-01-22&end=2028-09-01).
Similarly,
[kama sona on Discord](https://discord.com/invite/kama-sona-969386329513295872)
has just over 8,000 members, with 600-1,500 users reading and 50-100 users
messaging or voice chatting weekly. You can also
[check out its Insights dashboard](https://discord.com/developers/servers/969386329513295872/analytics/engagement?interval=2&start=2026-01-22&end=2028-09-01).
While Reddit does not provide the level of metrics detail that Discord does, we
can still see that [r/tokipona on Reddit](https://reddit.com/r/tokipona) has
33,000 subscribers, with 7,500 weekly readers and 400-600 weekly contributions.

Collecting all of these numbers into a confident estimate is hard. Each of these
different communities offer different ways of engaging with Toki Pona, and none
of these figures tell us whether any of those members or participants would call
themselves a Toki Pona speaker or whether any of them have used Toki Pona in
that cmmunity. We also don't know how much these communities overlap in
membership.

But still, we can estimate an upper bound: There can't be more than
20,000-30,000 Toki Pona speakers based on membership in the largest communities.
Further, Comparing to activity levels, about a tenth of that group (2,000-3,000
) are likely to be engaged enough to speak Toki Pona.

Putting all of these into some perspective, [ilo Muni](https://ilo.muni.la)
reports
[400-1000 monthly active authors](https://ilo.muni.la/?query=mi%2C+sina%2C+ona%2C+ni%2C+li%2C+e%2C+la%2C+o%2C+toki%2C+pona&scale=abs&field=authors&start=1627776000&unit=month),
or
[2000-4000 annually active authors](https://ilo.muni.la/?query=mi%2C+sina%2C+ona%2C+ni%2C+li%2C+e%2C+la%2C+o%2C+toki%2C+pona&scale=abs&field=authors&start=1627776000&unit=year)
over the most commonly used ten words.

### Everyone Else Who May Or May Not Speak Toki Pona

Of course, these statistics don't capture the _complete_ picture of the Toki
Pona community. Anecdotally, it is common to encounter people who speak Toki
Pona but do not participate in any of its major communities. An unknowable
number of people discover the language and begin learning it on their own or
with a friend, entirely offline. There are hundreds of personal websites and
blogs with pages in Toki Pona, or brief mentions of speaking the language.

YouTube videos like
[Oats Jenkins's "I Learned the World's Smallest Language"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kSl-iF9ZRLk&t=1s)
and the old version of
[Langfocus's "The world's TINIEST Language? (TOKI PONA)"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E4y7tf3VJAM)
have over a million views, while
[RobWords's "The world's smallest language"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PY3Qe_b9ufI),
or and
[jan Misali's "what is toki pona? (toki pona lesson one)"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2EZihKCB9iw),
or even silly videos like
[Oziji's "English with a toki pona accent"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gwBYGtUcxR4)
have reached several hundred thousand views. From these figures alone, we can
infer that well over a million people have at least _heard of_ Toki Pona.

In summary, while pinning down an exact number of Toki Pona speakers is hard, we
can say with confidence that there are several thousand speakers across the
world
