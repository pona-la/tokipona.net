## How long does it take to learn Toki Pona?

Like all languages, the amount of time needed to learn Toki Pona depends a lot on the person learning. It is difficult and unreliable to measure how long it takes people to reach certain levels of proficiency, and in turn, this is because it is difficult to measure Toki Pona proficiency. To make this point, there are many speakers who do not identify as fluent, but who are more skilled than many of those who do identify as fluent. Relatedly, different Toki Pona speakers will claim to have taken a different amount of time to reach any specific levels of proficiency.

It will definitely not take a weekend or a week. It's a common misconception that it is possible to learn Toki Pona to proficiency in such a short period of time. Most people could reasonably memorize the vocabulary and grammar within a week, and at that point they could begin to parse sentences. But they would not be able to understand most sentences without many more opportunities to see all the words in their myriad possible uses, and they would not be be ready to form sentences fluidly in a way others can understand.

The best estimate for how long it takes to learn Toki Pona comes from [the comprehensible input course _o pilin e toki pona_](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLwYL9_SRAk8EXSZPSTm9lm2kD_Z1RzUgm). This course asks you to listen to and watch one 20 minute video per day for 30 days. Through this course, [at least 14 people](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLwYL9_SRAk8F1hpZqv-pTgtnAjtD4yAy5) have achieved and demonstrated a conversational level of skill in Toki Pona. This is not necessarily a high level of proficiency, and this estimate only applies with certainty to this one method of learning Toki Pona, but it is reasonable to say that you can learn Toki Pona in 30 days with consistently applied effort. Still, this is only the start; as with any language, there is a long road ahead of you, and the learning never truly ends.
