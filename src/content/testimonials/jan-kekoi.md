My story begins with a really bad breakup.

I was devastated, I couldn’t stop thinking about what I could have done wrong,
why we broke up, what I could have done different; all things that were
extremely unhealthy for me. I needed something to break the obsessive pattern of
blaming myself and stewing in my own insecurities.

Then one day I saw a YouTube video on the smallest language ever invented.
Curious, I started looking into that minimalist language that is toki pona. I
had never learned an L2 before, and this seemed approachable and easy enough to
prepare myself for more practical languages, and most importantly, it stopped me
from thinking about my breakup.

Whenever the intrusive thoughts about how much I missed this person came about,
I would sit down and study toki pona. With time I stopped thinking about this
person all together, replaced with trying to understand the grammar and
inventing new combination glyphs.

As I delved more into the language, I began to see the community for what it
was. Vast, creative and diverse individuals that came together over a shared
love of language and curiosity. I may not consider myself a very active member
of this community, but I love it for what it is and I cannot wait to see what it
will become with time.

I now spend time making art and games using toki pona. For myself now, but one
day I’d like to share these things with the community. It’s funny how you can
lose one person, and earn an entire community from it.
