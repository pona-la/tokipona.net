Toki Pona came into my life during covid and offered a community, and I think that is what it offers most.

There are plenty of conlangs which offer other draws. They offer communication, a dream of a better international future, a cool fantasy or scifi world, intellectual stimulation. But few offer community in the same way that Toki Pona does.The Toki Pona community is a digital one. There are get-togethers but they are the exception, not the rule. Additionally the community does use the language but it is thoroughly bilingual. If the aim was to make Toki Pona the main language of the community - I think that ship has sailed.

But instead we have a community of art and fun. People who get together to share this little culture we have made for ourselves. To create whole new genres and express ourselves in new ways that will be appreciated by a small but passionate audience, hungry for any new experiences in the language.

And I think with community - Toki Pona offers culture. A culture of mutual respect, enjoyment and trust. One that holds up kindness and honesty amongst its main virtues. I think a perfect culture for autistic people.

For a short while Toki Pona (more specifically Luka Pona) was my main focus, but this time has passed. Yet still I remain within the community. I feel like a member of a diaspora, who has moved abroad but checks back in on my Toki Pona speaking family from time to time. I will not forget that as a community, you all are some of my most reliable supporters (in a mutual way).

I will continue to make some Toki Pona and Luka Pona content as time goes by as a gift of appreciation for that support, and as a way of inviting new people into this welcoming community that we have grown together.

*o toki pona. o kulupu pona. o pona :)*
