toki pona helps allow me to live a slower and more present life. It encouraged me to be more open-minded, decentralized from the Anglosphere, and more caring about others. Due to circumstances out of my control, I have less time than I'd like to work on maintaining my proficiency. 

Even so, I believe that toki pona can be for anyone. It's for those who

<ol type="a">
    <li> like to ponder "the ordinary" things around them</li>
    <li>want to simplify their thoughts and achieve inner-voice peace, and</li>
    <li>wish to meet an international community of speakers who all share these common goals.</li>
</ol>

toki pona is not easy to learn, despite what some may say. Sure, it may be easy **at first**, but as you learn more and more and interact with more people you realize it goes much deeper than one may expect. 

I'm someone with a lot of complex special interests and initially I found it hard to talk about them in toki pona* The more you ponder these complex topics, the more you will realize that *everything* is simple when broken down enough. It's like solving a puzzle. How *do* I say this thing I want to say? What does it mean *exactly*?

It's fun when you come up with an answer. Sometimes the answers are long, but don't be afraid of this. In toki pona*  you can describe topics almost like a story. These are the stories that make up our lives, one way or another.
