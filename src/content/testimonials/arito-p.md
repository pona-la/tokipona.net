Toki Pona, for me, means good language, one that unites through the simple
things of the whole, its commonalities, and not its opposites. I consider Toki
Pona useful for talking about universals (ideas that humans tend to have) and
for building microsystems of simple ideas (cogito, ergo, sum. And similar ones).

Toki Pona has meant a complete change in the way I interact with language and
the world, particularly impacting my career (pedagogy in Philosophy). It has
also helped me to focus less on complex thoughts, replacing them with simple
ones and not getting lost in unnecessary complexities. I use Toki Pona to reduce
complex and poorly formulated ideas into systems that are easy and convenient to
analyze, and to reinterpret them in order to develop new ideas or functions.
