i first learnt _toki pona_ in 2015 from a certain somewhat questionable resource
that i'm sure anyone who was around back then will remember. it was a real
transformation in the way language affects my life, being the first language i
learnt as an adult in which i became truly fluent, in the sense that i often
think in _toki pona_ and see the world in a different kind of way to how my
first languages (english and french) categorise it. objects feel more
meaningful, interpersonal interactions feel more comprehensible, when i think
about them in _toki pona_. i know this kind of hard sapir-whorf conception of
language affecting thought isn't popular nowadays, but as someone who is
autistic and prone to dissociation and psychosis, it definitely impacts my
experience of existence in a positive way.

_toki pona_ has also brought me together with a lot of people. not all of these
interactions have been good, and i don't engage with any of the big online
spaces any more despite spending a lot of time there previously, but i am very
glad for how it has created certain _kulupu_ for me irl. i speak it regularly in
person with several of my partners, and am doing my best to teach the others and
to reach out to other _ijo pona_ locally. _ale la, toki pona_ is connection, to
me. it's a new way of connecting my mind with the world outside it, and
communicating with others in ways that natural languages just can't match, and i
think that's pretty neat.
