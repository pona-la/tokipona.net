toki pona is a small language centered around empathy and understanding for the self and others. It's limited vocabulary, and analytical grammar means that we are often having to string together multiple nouns to refer to a single specific object. As a result, to talk about anything, we communicate so much about our perspective. This is why I fell in love with it 12 years ago. 

*wile mi ale la sina sona e mi la mi sona e sina. sina sona ala e mi la sina ken ala olin e mi li ken ala pana pona e mi.* (It is my utmost desire to have relationships built on understanding. Those who do not understand me cannot love me and cannot do well upon me.*)

Often times, I struggle to organize my thoughts when writing. This happens when a concept feels too big, and too vulnerable, and too complex. In those times I will typically communicate the thing in Toki Pona. *tenpo kama la, mi ken ante e one tawa toki Inli* (Later, I can translate it to English*).
