*toki pona* to me was a silly language I had no interest in. Then a friend at a language conference pulled me into a two hour *toki pona* workshop and I was HOOKED. 

For me it started off as a fun and silly way for me to say sneaky things to people in my close group. Then, I discovered the online *kulupu* (community). Now I use it daily, either to journal, talk to a few close friends, or just on online servers. 

*toki pona* has really opened my eyes to a new way of thinking and now that I see it, there is no going back. It has taught me to slow down, think about what details are important, and to think about what is really important in what I am trying to say. It lets you see the world from the eyes of who you are talking to. It encourages long meaningful conversation since context isn't always so clear until you get really get into it. This silly little language has now become a part of me and for better or for worse, I don't see it going anywhere.
