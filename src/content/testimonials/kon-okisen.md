the biggest benefit i got from learning toki pona was encouraging me to be more creative. a big part of that was the community itself making me feel like my creativity had value, but also i think the nature of toki pona itself requires you to be creative in expressing things out of necessity which i really enjoy. and also there’s a certain freedom to be i guess less self critical than writing in english for me, and the fact that sitelen pona and sitelen suwi feel more fun to write in than latin alphabet.

i learned toki pona in 2025 and was quite active in the community, teaching people on kama sona and working on a lot of creative projects. doing this, i felt kind of an emotional high from feeling i guess, significant? cool? unfortunately, i’ve let toki pona community and projects fall by the wayside during the last couple months because of school, but i still use it for journaling and art because i like the process of figuring out how to express a concept in toki pona. i do want to get back to the community though
