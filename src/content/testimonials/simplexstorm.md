Toki Pona is… weird; “*nasa*,” even. It has less than 200 words, which you’d think would make it impossible to communicate anything clearly, but that’s not what happens. It forces you to figure out exactly what something is to you: is music a form of entertaining sound, or one of sound-based entertainment?

Jokes aside, it feels great to learn. It works as a linguistic toy, a form of expression, a new lens to analyze the world with, or even a way to communicate with more people.
