To me, Toki Pona first and foremost is a friendly and tight-knit community. That's definitely what drew me in initially, even though I also like the language's principles and "design". I always enjoy contributing, even in smaller ways, to community projects and chatting with other members, both in English and Toki Pona. That is also how I mainly interact with it: by collaborating on group initiatives and hanging out in Discord.

I am the kind of person who always ends up tinkering with some side project, and in that sense, Toki Pona has enabled me to work on things that both I and others could appreciate, while encouraging me to try new skills. But what I find most valuable about the langauge is the countless ways in which you can engage with it.
