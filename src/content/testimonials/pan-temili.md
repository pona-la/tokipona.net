toki pona is a fun language to speak and explain to people at any level of linguistic knowledge. a graduate student will pick up on the logic of predicates and stacking prepositions immediately, and a layman will have an opportunity to learn new things about linguistics and potentially their own language, so that makes it a great learning and teaching aid for me.

toki pona is a great language for writing poetry in. there's no baggage of the literary tradition weighing me down, so i can just let words flow and do what just souds right to me. with time, i have developed a personal set of soft rules for poetry, in a nutshell, i'm doing my best of keeping rhythm intact and i use rhyming words for emphasis. it gets a bit more complicated, maybe i will write something about it for people to try out too.

toki pona is a great canvas for trying out conlanging. i've made a syllabic writing system for it, the whole process taught me a lot, now i'm a little more equipped when it comes to writing systems, be it learning an unfamiliar one or inventing one from scratch. another exercise i am considering is trying to evolve toki pona to the point of the introduction of a case system, i think that will push me to learn a lot that i'm missing right now.
