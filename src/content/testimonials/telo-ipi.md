I first discovered Toki Pona in 2023 through jan Misali on YouTube, specifically their original "12 Days of sona pi toki pona" series from 2015. Even though I thought I was pretty bad at learning languages, I decided I'd try to learn it just to see if I could. 

This idea of the language "simplifying your thoughts" seemed to be a bit of hoopla, but I soon discovered that yes, Toki Pona does indeed simplify your thoughts, and it is a very humbling and transformative lens to view the world through. The language can sound pretty silly, but I promise that it is a genuinely powerful tool that can and will change your perspective of reality.

In terms of the practicality of Toki Pona, I legitimately believe it to be a very effective conversational language. It is certainly also a good choice for a personal journal. I do not speak Toki Pona daily, as I do not always engage with other speakers, but I often think in the language. I also find it to be a particularly intriguing option for titling my musical works, as it opens the door for a multitude of interpretations, given that the language is so vague. I strongly encourage anyone and everyone to learn Toki Pona!
